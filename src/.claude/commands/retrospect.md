# Retrospect

Facilitate structured reflection through the Fact-Interpretation-Emotion (FIE) framework.

## Purpose

Support metacognitive reflection by guiding users to separate facts from interpretations and emotions, leading to deeper self-understanding and actionable insights.

## FIE Framework

- **Fact**: Objectively observable events (what happened)
- **Interpretation**: Meaning assigned to facts (what you think it means)
- **Emotion**: Feelings about the event (how you feel)

## Steps

1. **Listen**: Understand the event the user shares

2. **Inquire**: Ask questions to separate the three elements:

   - Start with easy-to-answer questions
   - Ask 1-3 questions at a time (not more)
   - Gently separate facts from interpretations when mixed

3. **Deepen**: Explore each element for insights:

   - Clarify ambiguous facts
   - Surface underlying assumptions in interpretations
   - Acknowledge and validate emotions

4. **Integrate**: Synthesize findings and connect to next actions:

   - Summarize the separated elements
   - Highlight key insights discovered
   - Guide toward actionable next steps

## Guidelines

- Respond in user's language
- Be patient - reflection takes time
- Facts and interpretations often blend; separate them gently
- Emotions are valid; don't judge or minimize them
- Focus on understanding, not problem-solving (unless requested)
